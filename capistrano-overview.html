MT. CAPISTRANO

Impasug-ong, Bukidnon · 1,834 meters above sea level

Destination

Overview

🏔️

The Mountain

Mt. Capistrano stands at 1,834 meters above sea level in Impasug-ong, Bukidnon. It is one of Mindanao's most accessible highlands, known for its cool climate, rolling meadows, and stunning panoramic views of the surrounding valleys.

🌊

The River

At the foot of Mt. Capistrano flows the Cagayan de Oro River — one of Mindanao's major waterways. The river carves through dramatic gorges and rapids, making it the premier destination for white-water rafting in the Philippines.

📍

Location

Located in Impasug-ong, Bukidnon, Mt. Capistrano is approximately 2–3 hours from Cagayan de Oro City and is easily reachable by private vehicle or organized tours from the city.

Things To Do

Activities

🚣

White-Water Rafting

The Cagayan de Oro River offers some of the Philippines' most thrilling white-water rafting. Navigate through Class II to IV rapids across an 8–14 km stretch of gorges, cliffs, and rushing waters. Suitable for beginners and experienced rafters alike — guides and safety equipment are always provided.

⭐ Must Try ₱500–₱1,200
🛶

Kayaking

For a more peaceful experience, kayaking along calmer stretches of the river lets you take in the lush canyon walls, rock formations, and surrounding forest at your own pace. Ideal for families and those who prefer a gentler water adventure.

Family Friendly ₱300–₱600
🥾

Mountain Trekking

Hike through pine-covered trails leading to the summit viewpoint of Mt. Capistrano. The trek rewards you with sweeping views of Bukidnon's highland plateau and on clear days, distant mountain ranges. Beginner to intermediate difficulty.

Nature ₱100–₱300
⛺

Camping

Spend a night under the stars on the highland meadows of Mt. Capistrano. The cool mountain air and minimal light pollution create perfect conditions for stargazing and campfire evenings with friends and family.

Overnight ₱150–₱250 / pax

Conservation

Environmental Importance

💧

Watershed Protection

Mt. Capistrano is a critical watershed area that feeds the Cagayan de Oro River system, supplying water to communities across Misamis Oriental and Bukidnon.

🦜

Biodiversity Hotspot

The forests around Mt. Capistrano shelter a rich variety of endemic flora and fauna, including rare bird species, Philippine deer, and native orchids found nowhere else in the world.

🌱

Carbon Sink

The dense pine and tropical forests act as a vital carbon sink, absorbing CO₂ and helping regulate the regional climate — keeping Bukidnon's highlands notably cooler than surrounding lowlands.

🌾

Indigenous Land

The mountain and surrounding highlands are ancestral domains of the Matigsalug Manobo people. Preserving this land means protecting both a natural ecosystem and living indigenous cultural heritage.

Before You Go

Visitor Tips

🧥

Bring Warm Clothes

Temperatures at the summit can drop to 10–15°C especially at night and early morning. Pack a jacket or hoodie even if it's sunny in the city.

🕖

Go Early

Start your trek or river activity by 7:00–8:00 AM. Afternoons can bring fog, rain, and rough river conditions, especially during the wet season (June–October).

🧴

Pack Essentials

Bring sunscreen, insect repellent, a refillable water bottle, trail snacks, and waterproof bags — especially for rafting where your belongings will get wet.

🚌

How to Get There

From Cagayan de Oro City, take a bus or van to Impasug-ong (2–3 hrs). Organized rafting tours often include transport from CDO. Private vehicles are the most flexible option.

🌿

Leave No Trace

Help preserve the mountain's natural beauty. Carry out all your trash, avoid picking plants, and stay on designated trails. Respect the land and its indigenous stewards.

📵

Limited Signal

Mobile signal is weak to none at the summit and along some river stretches. Download offline maps before you go and inform someone of your plans before heading out.
